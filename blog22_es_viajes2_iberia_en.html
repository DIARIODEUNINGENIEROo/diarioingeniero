August 18 to 22, 2021

Dia 1, visitas por el Bierzo


I leave my town, Albares de la Ribera and head to Bembibre, where I visit the Ecce Homo sanctuary. This church is more than 5 centuries old and previously there was another temple there, making it one of the oldest churches in all of Bierzo. I had never visited, having been to Bembibre more than 200 times, because it was long before the current city.
Bembibre is the capital of Bierzo Alto and has no more than 10,000 inhabitants. However, it has almost all services and Ponferrada is a stone's throw away, just 13 kms. Due to the large number of large towns around, summers are filled with people, especially on weekend afternoons and nights, with a large number of bars and nightlife areas.
Then I was going to see the Mozarabic church of Peñalba de Santiago but because they were restoring the road I had to choose an alternative route, which left me with Lake Carucedo and the Cornatel castle. This castle is one of the oldest Bercian castles and is located on top of a mountain where you can see Lake Carucedo and Ponferrada in the distance, where one of the largest and oldest castles in Spain is located, of the order of the templars
To do this, I will first visit Priaranda del Bierzo, a very small town with a lot of charm, with the typical mountain houses of Bierzo. Then I continue to the castle of Cornatel where you can see the lake that I wanted to reach. At the end I will arrive at the hermitage of Lago de Carucedo where there is a path that leads to an area where one can bathe. At the end is the aquatic area where there are several kayak boats.

Dia 2, Lugo bajo


The next visit will be to the northwest, first stop in Quiroga where I go to the recently restored and expanded geological museum where you can see the skeleton of a cave bear and mammoth bones found in the area. . In addition to information on the location of the main prospections.
Then I go to Monforte de Lemos where the most admirable thing is the inn located at the top of the mountain, next to the monastery, with excellent views of the city. This site was an old fortress that would be the point chosen for the city due to the quality of the land near the river. Here we can find the Lemos County Palace.
I continue along the road to the north and arrive at Samos, where one of the largest monasteries of the entire Camino de Santiago route is located. Surrounded by great nature and with numerous hiking trails around. Next to this monastery is the city of Sarría, which is just the next point on the Camino de Santiago. With a well-kept old town, numerous restaurants and hostels for pilgrims.
After seeing this town, I continue north to Meira and the great temple of Santa María with a huge square and the town hall next to it. Very close to this town is the source of the Miño River, which is nothing more than a large number of rocks in front of a very fertile land where a large number of flowers abound.
Following a fairly difficult regional road through the mountains, I arrive in the afternoon at Mondoñedo, one of the towns with the most ecclesiastical tradition, with its old cathedral made entirely of rock in a style very close to the cathedral. from Santiago. There is the episcopal palace.
Finally I will arrive at Lorenzana.

Day 3, Lugo Alto


I leave Lorenzana bound for the Basilica of San Martiño in Foz. It is considered the oldest cathedral in Spain and dates from the 9th century. It has vegetable ornaments on the capitals and walls decorated with polychrome paintings, which are well preserved and some have been restored.
After seeing this basilica, I will go along the coast to visit the beach of the Cathedrals. This beach has the characteristic of having rock formations on the sand that seem to make figures. It is visited by hundreds of thousands of people every year, so there is a capacity limit.
Continuing along the same road, I arrived at Ribadeo, one of the most beautiful fishing villages in Lugo, with a very unique square and palaces and incredible views of the sea and the estuary. I also visited the street of Indian houses where there are mansions of people who made a lot of money in America two centuries ago and had the most extravagant houses built. Before leaving Ribadeo I visited the illa Pancha lighthouse at the end of the city with very nice views of the sea.
In the afternoon, already in Lorenzana where I had my accommodation, I visited the city's church, which has deficiencies and will have to be restored for so many years. After seeing the central square, visit the Tovar fortress, an old stately palace.

Day 4, Asturias in 1 day


I leave Lorenzana very early and drive along the A8, the north road towards Asturias. Just as I passed through Ribadeo I entered Asturias. I visit Luarca, one of the most beautiful fishing villages in Asturias along with Cudillero. Apart from its beautiful streets, and its old buildings, we have a very good viewpoint, and tremendous views from the lighthouse. There are also two very large public car parks on the outskirts of town, one with several floors and the other in a very large area.
I decided to visit towns in the interior of Asturias since Avilés already knew it. Take the N-634 to Salas, a town with a very pretty square and castle and an old church that together gave it a fairly medieval air. The road is not good and most of the time do not exceed the speed of 70 km/h, never going out of fourth gear. Road with many curves and all the time in the shade covered by trees. After Salas I stopped at Cornellana and saw the ruins of an old monastery. In that small stop take the opportunity to eat. Then I went to Grade, one of the towns that surprised me on this trip, with its colorful houses, its town hall and square, and the town's old chapel which, thanks to the head of the tourist office, I was able to look inside her. It had the remains of the wall that left this building together with the wall and other buildings such as the Miranda-Valdecarzana palace.
After passing through this town, I'll set off towards Oviedo, which was one of the first cities with history that I visited as a child, in high school. First visit the churches of Santa María del Naranco and the church of San Miguel de Lillo at the top of the city. I was also able to see how the view has changed with the inclusion of that large white building by Calatrava, Palacio de Congresos, which does a lot of damage to the view. From my point of view, it stands out a lot and makes you not pay attention to what is truly important in your eyes.
When I finish I go down to the city and leave the car very close to the Santuyano park where the third pre-Romanesque church in the city is located, San Julián de Prados. Continuing along the path, I will arrive at the historic center where the first thing I do is visit the provincial archaeological museum that is located in the cloister of the Monastery of San Vicente. This monastery was one of the oldest and one of the first buildings in the city at the end of the Visigoth period. Very close to the museum is the Plaza de Feijoo and the church of Santa María de la Corte. Then I will go to the cathedral, where curiously the day I went there was an exhibition of regional dances that gave a lot of play to that Oviedo of yesteryear. Among the most outstanding palaces in the town would be the Valacio de Velarde, the Valdecarzana palace, the Camposagrado palace. Other important buildings are the Royal Institute of Asturian Studies and the University of Oviedo and its courtyard.
To finish a visit to the Campo de San Francisco, the Plaza Escandalera, the Campoamor theater, the visit to the Plaza de la Constitución and the town hall, the church of San Isidoro and further on the basilica of San Juan el Real, a church of greater realengo since if it did not have a cathedral it could well be considered.

Once I have seen Oviedo, I will stop for 1 hour in Mieres to take a walk through the old town, and see as the most important elements the high school and the house of culture as well as a Rosaceo mansion that are similar in architecture colonial type. The church of San Juan also has that most typical look of South America.
I will continue through the toll until I arrive and I will leave through León to cross the province a little to the north and shorten the road, however, it is not very good since there are many curves but the landscape in case of going during the day is surely worth it.

Day 5, from Albares to Guadalajara, important towns on the A6


I leave Albares on my way to Guadalajara and in between I take the opportunity to see one of the most important and historic towns through which the A6 passes, the highway that connects Madrid with La Coruña. We start with Benavente, the second municipality with the most inhabitants in the province of Zamora. This city's most representative elements are the town hall square, the church of San Juan del Mercado next door, the castle of Benavente with its snail tower, now a national inn. This tower has inside one of the best preserved Mudejar coffered ceilings, which was transferred from the sanctuary of San Román del Valle in 1970 and dates from the 15th century and the Romanesque church of Santa María del Azogue. Next to the parador there is a large park where Solita's house is, which is very large and you can enjoy beautiful views of the entire plain.

After visiting Benavente, I will depart for Tordesillas a very important city historically because the Treaty of Tordesillas was signed between Portugal and Spain for the distribution of land in the conquest of America. Proof of this are the Houses of the Treaty where there is an exhibition of models of ships and documents and maps related to the treaty.
Of great importance is the monastery of Santa Clara, ordered to be built by Alfonso XI in 1370 and the place where Juana La Loca, the mother of Carlos V, lived after the communal revolt. A Gothic church is important and as a jewel of the monastery a wooden coffered ceiling with decorative elements in the form of pine cones in the Mudejar style in the main chapel. There are also Arab baths in good condition, very similar to those found in Andalusia.
Important churches would be Santa María, of very large dimensions, San Pedro, which impressed me due to the number of heraldic shields and details since a nobleman commanded to create a pantheon and bury his and San Antolín's relatives. It also highlights the Roman bridge of the city.
After Tordesillas I will stop at Medina del Campo, where just to visit its castle is worth a visit. The highlight is its large Plaza Mayor, where we can find the large collegiate church of San Antolin, the town hall and the royal testamentary palace attached to the town hall. Very close to the admiral's palace, the main street and the church of San Miguel. Of great importance are the royal butcher shops, a completely restored building that has existed for centuries. Further from the center there would already be the Dueñas Palace, the Santa Clara Monastery, and the Mota Castle, gigantic in size, which due to the time of day I could only see from the outside. Together with the castles of Coca and Cuellar, which are also in Castilla y León, they seem to me to be the most impressive castles in Spain.
