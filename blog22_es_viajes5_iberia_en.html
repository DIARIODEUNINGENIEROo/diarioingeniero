1 a 4 de octubre de 2021

Day 1, Toledo to Merida


I left Toledo in the morning on my way to Mérida, the first large city and the most monumental of the trip together with Cáceres and perhaps one of the cities with the most history in Spain, whose streets hide great treasures from Roman times under their floors. or later. This city was chosen by the Roman elites to retire and spend their last years of life. Merida comes from emerita and means retired.

Mérida is famous for its Roman theater and amphitheatres, but there are many other attractions to this city. I visited it when I was little and I just remember these places. On this visit I didn't spend a single euro on monuments and I took the whole day very different from the visit when I was little in which I would stop for no more than four hours and see four different sites.
Park the car not far from the Roman theater and without paying 1 euro since I left it in a residential area very close to a supermarket. I would start with what is close to the theater, such as the Roman museum where the original figures of both the amphitheater and the theater are. It also keeps many objects found in excavations throughout the autonomous community of Extremadura. Many mosaics of Roman residences are displayed on the walls of this museum which, in addition to being large, is quite tall, three floors and almost 20 meters high.

It is also divided into Roman times and has a large collection of coins and vessels from the time.

I would visit the obelisk of Santa Eulalia, the basilica that is quite impressive on the inside, but don't go to the crypt, which requires a fee and keeps one of the oldest images of this saint.
Later, towards the center, I would visit the Roman forum in which the portico is preserved, the temple of Diana whose square has been recently restored, the arch of Trajan as one of the main Roman remains.
Visit the citadel and the Visigothic museum that is inside a convent as monuments from other times. Also the Merida parador.
As religious buildings the co-cathedral and the church of Carmen and the cathedral square.
After seeing the entire center I would go to the outskirts through the river park where I saw the aqueduct of Los Milagros. One of the longest preserved aqueducts in Spain and the most unknown since being on the outskirts few know about it. With this, I would go on my way to the hotel north of Mérida, which would allow me to see the province of Cáceres better, since this trip, except for Mérida, was focused on the province of Cáceres.

Day 2 Montanchez to Trujillo


The first point to visit in the morning is the town of Montanchez, whose castle can be visited completely and is located at the top of the town. The town is quite common to other towns in the area, with a fairly old church, El Salvador, and a recently restored main square.

The second town, Alcuescar, is somewhat larger than the first. It has a larger church than the one in Montanchez, that of the Asunción. But what stands out the most is having the Church of Santa Lucía del Trampal just 3 km from the town. One of the few churches in all of Spain still standing from the Visigoth period. It is a mix between Visigothic and Mozarabic and because it was hidden in the undergrowth for centuries, it has been preserved in very good condition. It was discovered by a looter who was looking for remains of the French war of independence and came across this construction. Then, before warning the people, the entire area would be cleaned and the first archaeological works carried out. All the remains of this church are in the archaeological museum of Cáceres. Although they have built a building to accommodate a guard and make an explanation of the building as well as a visual projection.

After seeing these two towns, I would see Valdefuentes, very close to the hotel where I stayed, which is just 30km from Cáceres. This town has a very large square and a couple of churches, as well as a large palace of the nobility of that time, today owned by the town hall.

In the afternoon after eating I went to see Guadalupe , and her monastery that although I had visited it when I was a child I did not remember practically anything. The entrance to the monastery church is free and the entrance to the monastery is 5 euros. It is well worth entering as they show you rooms that are authentic cultural gems, full of paintings and precious stones. And it is that Guadalupe for many years has been a place of pilgrimage to the Virgin and gold and jewels have been donated from America. The cloister is one of the largest in Spain, having a large garden, and this monastery came to house the lives of 400 friars. The town also has quite a homogeneity in its buildings, with a cobblestone floor and streets with a multitude of historical arches.

After seeing the monastery, I would go to Trujillo, one of the prettiest and most historic towns in Cáceres and I would dare to say in all of Spain. Such is the density of monuments per square meter, the majority being palaces and religious buildings that it has been chosen along with Cáceres to shoot the mega-film Game of Thrones there and has been re-elected to shoot numerous scenes for the new series, the prequel to Game of Thrones, a series consisting of 10 seasons, one of the longest. This town had a fairly large citadel in Arab times but its splendor would come thanks to the Spanish conquerors in America. The most famous conqueror is Francisco Pizarro, who has a large palace in the central square and a statue on horseback that is one of the most photographed in Spain. The square is surrounded by palaces since many of his men were from Trujillo and also the cradle of other conquerors. The Palace of the Conquest is the most visited, with a large coat of arms on the corner of the building and that of San Carlos. Churches in the square, San Martín, and others such as Santiago and Santa María la Mayor. In that area there are several defensive towers and walls that give the city that medieval air. It also has more churches further from the center and convents that increase the monumentality of the city. I was only 3 hours but this town is well worth a whole day to visit it.

Day 3, Cáceres


The main dish of the trip is a visit to Cáceres, which together with Mérida are World Heritage cities.
Cáceres is a walled city full of palaces and beautiful squares and tall towers like the one in Bujaro that leads into the old town. The Plaza Mayor is one of the largest squares in Spain and one of the most photographed. The Palacio de los Carvajales, the Palacio de los Golfines de Abajo, which has been open to visitors for not many years (the one above is a restaurant), that of the Counts of Adanero, the house of the vanes, etc. As the most important churches, the church of San Francisco, whose two towers can be climbed and have the best views of the city. Also noteworthy are the weapons museum and the archaeological museum that is located inside a palace (the house of weather vanes) that was built from an old Arab palace that has a well-preserved Arab cistern. Other of the most important palaces are that of the commander of Alcuescar, that of Montezuma, that of Galarza and that of the Marquis of Camarena, somewhat far from the center.

As important religious buildings apart from the church of San Martin and the co-cathedral, there is the church of San Mateo with its high tower, that of Santo Domingo, that of Santiago el Mayor, that of San Juan. Convents have that of San Pablo in the historic center and that of San Francisco with a very large doorway, somewhat on the outskirts. Also the hermitage in the tower of Bujaro in the center.

On the outskirts of the city I went up at the end of the afternoon to see the sunset from the sanctuary of the Virgen de la Montaña. This location has the best views of the entire city of Cáceres.

Day 4, de Medellin to Toledo


Starting with Almoharín, a town with a square and streets typical of the area.

Then I would go to see Medellin as the main course of the day, the birthplace of the best Spanish conqueror in America, Hernan Cortes, conqueror of the entire Valley of Mexico. This city is of Roman origin and the Roman circus and Roman columns and remains have recently been recovered. The importance of the Roman city comes from the great bridge that crosses the river. After the Romans, the Arabs would come and build a great fortification on top of the town. Apart from the old area of ​​the Alcazar, Medellin has a beautiful square with the statue of Hernan Cortes and two or three important churches. Unlike Trujillo, Hernan Cortes hardly lived in the city, since he would live in Mexico and marry a native, so he did not continue with buildings like those of the other town and there was no lineage of conquerors in the town.

Then, I would finally visit Villanueva de la Serena, which although it is a modern city, one of the most inhabited in Extremadura and that after the merger with Don Benito became the third most populous city, it has quite a history.
It has a fairly large church, a large town hall square and some palace in the center.
